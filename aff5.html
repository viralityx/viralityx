What to say

Steal these. They sound like a person, not an ad.

When they're complaining about being busy

"You know how you're always saying you miss calls when you're out on a job? I know a guy who fixes exactly that."

The personal angle (strongest one)

"I'm using them for my business. They set up the systems so I stop losing work. Want me to introduce you?"

The soft opener, no pressure

"They'll run a free scan on your business and show you where you're leaking money. No pitch, just a report. Want the link?"

The lazy version that still works

"Text this guy, tell him I sent you." (then send us their name)

What NOT to say

Four rules, and you're golden

  • Don't quote prices. Every deal is different. If they ask what it costs, say "depends on what they find, that's what the call is for."
  • Don't promise results. Never "they'll double your business." Just share your own experience.
  • Don't try to explain the tech. You don't have to know how it works. That's our job.
  • Don't sell. Seriously. You're making an introduction between two people you like. That's all.
