60-Second Pre-Call Brief

Read this before you dial

Who he is: Nick runs At Home Services, a Pella window and door installer in a small, booming Kentucky town (new Ford plant, thousands of jobs moving in). He's a 20-year friend of Josh, an action-taker who gets analysis paralysis, and once he's sold, he's all in. He was the very first person to run our scan.

His one hangup: the $3,500 upfront. He told Josh straight up he can't come off that cash while making payroll, even knowing it works. So do NOT lead with a setup fee. Lead with the outcome and a low-risk way in, same lean base plus performance structure we just used to close Rob.

The real prize (bigger than Nick): Nick is a super-connector in a town Josh wants to own, and his wife just launched a community and business podcast. Land Nick, deliver a fast win, and he becomes a referral hub and the podcast becomes a lead channel into the whole town. This is Josh's hometown thesis. Nick is the doorway.

Don't over-sell or over-explain the tech. He's a hands-on tradesman drowning in decisions. Make it simple, make it low-risk, give him one clear next step.

BOTH
Question Bank (open-ended, mapped to intel we need)
1. STORY & VISION
We need: what he installs, where he wants the business, appetite to grow.
  • "How'd you get At Home Services going, and what all do you handle, just windows and doors or more?"
  • "With the Ford plant and everybody moving in, what does that mean for your business?"Huge tailwind. If he's hungry to capture the boom, lead with lead-gen.
  • "Where do you want this in a couple years? Bigger crew, steady, or just less stress?"
2. HOW JOBS COME IN — CORE
We need: lead sources, volume, speed-to-lead, missed calls.
  • "Where do most of your jobs come from right now, referrals, past customers, online?"
  • "When somebody calls while you're up on a job, what happens to that call?"Missed-call leak. In home services the first responder usually wins. Text-back + speed-to-lead wedge.
  • "Roughly how many new inquiries a month, and how many turn into jobs?"
3. QUOTES & FOLLOW-UP
We need: the estimate process and what happens to quotes that don't close.
  • "Walk me through how you quote a window or door job. How long from first call to a number in their hands?"
  • "When someone gets a quote and goes quiet, how do you follow up?"Windows are big-ticket, long consideration. Follow-up sequence + financing reminders = closed jobs sitting on the table.
  • "Do you offer financing? Do people know about it?"
4. REVIEWS & REPUTATION
We need: is his reputation being captured, how strong locally.
  • "In a town like yours, your name is everything. Do you have any system for getting reviews after a job?"
  • "When people look you up, what do they find?"Review engine wedge, plus feeds the connector play.
5. HIS NETWORK & THE PODCAST — THE PRIZE
We need: how connected he is, and the podcast as a lead channel.
  • "You know everybody in town. How much of your work is you just knowing the right people?"
  • "Tell me about the podcast your wife started. Who's listening?"Community + local business owners. This is a referral and ad-read lead channel into the whole town.
  • "If we made you money and you were happy, are you the type who'd send other business owners our way?"If yes, he's your affiliate hub. Set up the referral engine.
6. TIME, ADMIN & THE MONEY REALITY
We need: what buries him, and his cash reality (to tee up the lean deal).
  • "What part of running the business eats your nights and weekends?"
  • "If the phone, the follow-ups, and the reviews just handled themselves, what would you do with that time?"
  • "Cash-flow wise, what does a comfortable way to start look like for you?"Let him tell you the money wall. Then hand him the lean base + performance answer.
The Close — Magic Wand

Save the most important question for last. Ask ONE. Then shut up.

"If you had a magic wand and could fix or automate one thing in the business tomorrow, what would it be?"
  • "What's the one headache that, if it disappeared, would change your week?"
  • "Of everything we talked about, what do you need the most help with right now?"
For Nick it's likely "more steady jobs without me doing the marketing" or "get the phone and paperwork off my plate." Whatever he says is the headline of the proposal. Write it word for word.

On The Call

DO

  • Let Josh's relationship carry the warmth. It's a huge head start.
  • Lead with the outcome: more jobs, less admin, more time with his wife.
  • Remove the money wall early with the lean, low-risk structure.
  • Open the connector and podcast door, that's the real prize.
  • Give him one simple next step. He needs a nudge, not a menu.

DON'T

  • Lead with a $3,500 setup fee. He will freeze. That's his exact hangup.
  • Overwhelm him with tech or too many options (analysis paralysis).
  • Be salesy with a friend. Josh's trust is the asset, protect it.
  • Forget to ask about the podcast and his network. It's the whole play.
  • Promise a specific dollar number live. Gather, then model.
Closing Script
"Nick, this is exactly what we needed. Here's what's next. We'll put together a simple, low-risk way to get started, built so you see more jobs coming in before you ever spend real money. No big upfront wall. Let's get one more quick call on the calendar and walk you through it."
