Section 01

What they actually do (in plain language)

Turf USA is a wholesale supplier of synthetic turf, ivy products, and installation materials. They sell to installers and retailers across Florida out of two warehouses (Boca Raton flagship, Tampa new-ish hub). Their customers are not homeowners. Their customers are the guys who install turf in homeowner backyards. That's the entire business.

They've been around long enough to carry a 15-year warranty and have a real footprint. They're not a startup. They're an established regional operator in a market that's growing 12.5% a year through 2031.

Section 02

The strategic angle you lead with

This is a wholesale operator riding a structural growth wave (artificial turf is $7.31B globally in 2026, North America = 38% of that). The macro is good. The problem is the operational infrastructure their dealer book depends on is leaking dealers to competitors with better digital systems (GreenPRO, ProGreen, Synthetic Turf International all have dealer programs Turf USA doesn't).

Frame it operator to operator: "You're in a category that's about to compound for the next decade. The wholesalers who win this run are the ones whose dealer book doesn't leak out the back door. Right now your back door has a few holes worth talking about."

Section 05

Industry-specific hooks (only mention if natural)

  • JavaScript-rendered site. turfusa.com is a single-page app that requires JS to render. Bad for SEO. Dealers searching "wholesale synthetic turf Boca" are finding competitors before they find Turf USA.
  • No bilingual presence. South Florida has a heavy Spanish-speaking installer base. No Spanish-language sales line or site variant visible. Local competitors who do this are winning that segment quietly.
  • No public dealer portal. GreenPRO, ProGreen, Synthetic Turf International all have dealer programs front and center. Turf USA's dealer program (if it exists) is invisible from the outside. Dealers evaluate visible loyalty programs.
  • Two locations, no unified CRM visible. Boca and Tampa appear to operate as two phone numbers, one shared sales@ inbox, and not much else tying them together.
  • Sample fulfillment is a black box. Installers need samples in 48 hours to close their own jobs. No visible sample-request workflow on the site.

Worth 20 minutes to see what we'd add?
