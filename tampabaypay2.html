Who he is: Zac built Tampa Bay Pay into the highest-rated payments team in Tampa Bay. 250+ merchants, 5.0 rating across 157+ reviews, a 10-person team, bilingual support. He already resells websites, SEO, and GoHighLevel, and runs his own 8-step consultative sales process. He is not a beginner.

Tailwind to mention: 60%+ of SMBs now run dual pricing, his lead model, but VA and MN "all-in pricing" laws are reshaping it, so compliance is a live opening.

Do NOT pitch website fixes, missed-call recovery, or review generation as the headline. His marketing and reputation are already excellent. He sells that stuff himself. Respect it or you lose credibility in 30 seconds.

Question Bank (open-ended, mapped to intel we need)
1. STORY & VISION
We need: where the book is, where he wants it, grow vs sell vs diversify.
  • "Walk us through Tampa Bay Pay. How'd you get from your first merchant to 250-plus?"
  • "What are you most proud of building?"
  • "Where do you want this in two or three years? Bigger book, sell the portfolio, or build out the value-add side?"This answer decides whether we lead with growth, retention/valuation, or the channel.
2. THE BOOK & HOW HE WINS MERCHANTS
We need: portfolio size/volume, lead sources, the sales engine, throughput limits.
  • "How many merchants are you actively processing for right now, and how does new business come in? Referrals, partners, outbound?"
  • "Walk me through the rate analysis. When a merchant sends a statement, what actually happens and who does it?"If it's manual / per-rep → AI statement-analysis engine is the headline build.
  • "How long from statement to proposal? How many can a rep realistically work in a week?"
  • "When a merchant uploads a statement or books a consult, how fast does someone get to them?"
3. RETENTION & ATTRITION
We need: attrition rate, how he sees churn coming, what a lost merchant costs.
  • "What does attrition look like for you? Do merchants ever leave, and do you usually see it coming?"
  • "Do you have any way to flag a merchant whose volume is dropping or who's gone quiet, before they cancel?"If "no" → retention engine + health scoring. Big residual and valuation lever.
  • "When you think about selling or valuing the book someday, how much does retention factor in?"If he cares about portfolio value → tie everything to stickiness and the 3-5x vs 1-2x multiple.
4. THE VALUE-ADD SIDE & THE CHANNEL
We need: what he resells, how it performs, appetite to offer AI ops to his book.
  • "You already do websites, SEO, GoHighLevel for merchants. How's that side going for you?"
  • "Have you thought about offering your merchants the AI stuff, voice agents, review automation, CRM? They trust you, and almost none of them have it."This is the partnership door. If he leans in → frame ViralityX as his white-label engine.
  • "Would a value-add bundle that makes merchants stickier be interesting, given what stickiness does to your residual and your book's value?"
5. TEAM & SUPPORT LOAD
We need: team size/roles, where time goes, how the support promise scales.
  • "Your whole brand is the direct line, no 800 number. As the book grows, how's the team holding up on support?"
  • "Where does your week go? What are you doing that you wish you weren't?"
  • "If support ran itself for the simple stuff, where would you put that time?"
6. TECH STACK
We need: what they run so we integrate, not replace. We know GHL already.
  • "You're on GoHighLevel, same as us. What else are you running for CRM, residual tracking, onboarding?"
  • "What works, and what do you fight with?"
The Close — Magic Wand

Save the most important question for last. Ask ONE. Then shut up.

"If you had a magic wand and could fix or automate one thing in the business tomorrow, what would it be?"
  • "What's the one thing that, if it worked the way you wanted, would change the game for Tampa Bay Pay?"
  • "Of everything we covered, what do you need the most help with right now?"
Ask it, then go completely silent. Whatever he says first is the headline of the proposal. Write it word for word.

On The Call

DO

  • Open by acknowledging his reviews and team. He built something real.
  • Talk his language: residuals, attrition, portfolio value, basis points.
  • Use the shared GoHighLevel stack to build rapport.
  • Probe both doors: client work and the channel partnership.
  • Ask, then go quiet. Book the follow-up before you hang up.

DON'T

  • Pitch website, SEO, missed calls, or reviews as the headline. He sells those.
  • Talk down to him or over-explain AI. He's sophisticated.
  • Quote a price. This is discovery.
  • Promise a specific dollar number live. Gather, then model.
  • Open random new ideas. Stay on his book and his goals.
