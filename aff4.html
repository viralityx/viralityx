Part A · Onboarding a new affiliate
Target: 10 minutes, start to finish. If it takes longer, the process is broken.
1

Create their unique link

Naming convention: virality-x.com/name (e.g. /nick, /vanessa, /calvin). It must be visibly theirs, they should be able to look at it and know it's their link.

2

Create the affiliate record in the CRM

Name, contact, their link/code, payout method, assigned salesperson, date onboarded. This record is the source of truth for attribution.

3

Wire the notification

Lead comes in through their link → auto-text/email the assigned salesperson with the affiliate's name attached. Format: "New lead via Nick — [name], [contact]. Reach out now."

4

Send the W-9 immediately

Before their first payout, not at tax time. Collect it during onboarding while they're excited. No W-9, no payout.

5

Send the kit

Signed Referral Partner Agreement + Welcome Kit. Podcast Kit too if they have an audience (Vanessa, Calvin).

6

Add them to the payout tracker

So earnings accumulate against the $600 threshold from day one.

Part B · Lead → close → payout
The backbone. Every affiliate lead runs this exact path.
1

Lead fires

Through their link or a direct text intro. Auto-notification hits the assigned salesperson with the affiliate name attached.

2

Contact fast

See SLA below. This is the whole game, an affiliate lead is warm because someone they trust vouched for us. Waste that and you waste the affiliate too.

3

Tag it properly

Source = affiliate name. Assigned rep. Status. Do this at intake, not later. If it's not tagged, the affiliate doesn't get paid and you'll have an argument on your hands.

4

Run the normal flow

Qualify → discovery → proposal → close. Standard playbook. The affiliate has zero role here, that's the whole point.

5

Close + first payment clears

Mark "affiliate payable" in the tracker with the close date and the fee owed.

6

Pay within 15 days

Net-15 from the client's first payment clearing. Their chosen method. Log the paid date.

7

Tell the affiliate

Don't just silently send money. Text them: "Your guy signed. $500 is on the way." That's what makes them go find the next one.

SLA — CONTACT WITHIN 1 HOUR (same business day at the absolute latest).
An affiliate referral is a favor someone did us with their own reputation. If we sit on it for two days, we don't just lose the lead, we lose the affiliate's confidence and they stop sending. Speed is the entire product here.

Part C · Attribution rules & edge cases
Decide these now, not in an argument later. The CRM record governs, always.
Duplicate referral

Two partners refer the same business? First one on record wins. Timestamp decides. No splitting.

Already in our pipeline

If the business is already in our CRM in any status before the affiliate submits them, it doesn't count. No fee. This protects us from someone "referring" a deal we already sourced.

Self-referrals

An affiliate referring their own business, or one they own, is not eligible. (Nick is a client AND an affiliate, his own account earns him nothing.)

Bigger builds

$500 is the standard. If a referral turns into something large (a Pella-level account, an enterprise build), we agree a higher number in writing before it closes, never after. Don't let them find out later and feel shorted.

Referral closes after they quit

Anything referred before they left still pays if it closes within 90 days. Per the agreement.

Dispute

The CRM source record decides it. Period. No record, no fee, which is exactly why Part A step 3 matters.

Compliance & watch-outs

Don't get these wrong

  • The 1099 threshold is $600 a year, not $6,000. Two closed referrals puts Vanessa over it. Collect the W-9 at onboarding and watch the YTD column. Getting this wrong is an IRS problem, not a paperwork problem.
  • FTC disclosure is mandatory for anyone with an audience. Vanessa and Calvin must say they're paid partners on every read. It's in their agreement and their kit. If they skip it, that's exposure for them and for us. Spot-check the episodes.
  • Speed is the product. A cold affiliate lead doesn't just die, it kills the affiliate's willingness to send another. One-hour SLA, no exceptions.
  • Pay fast and tell them. The first payout is the moment this program either becomes real to them or quietly dies. Make the first one land early and loudly.
  • Don't over-promise the pipeline. These are friends of Josh. If we take their referrals and fumble them, we damage Josh's relationships, not just ours.
