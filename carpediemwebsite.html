What's Wrong with the Current Site

Every issue below is costing Paul potential founding members

1

Wrong Identity

The page title reads "GivBux Lounge" — not Carpe Diem Society. Before a visitor even sees the content, the browser tab is telling them they're at the wrong place. This creates immediate brand confusion.

New build: Brand name in every touchpoint — tab title, hero, nav, footer
2

No Value Proposition

There's no headline explaining what Carpe Diem Society is, why it exists, or why anyone should care. The page jumps straight from a logo to a photo of furniture. Visitors have to guess what this is.

New build: Hero with tagline, subtitle, and animated stat counters
3

No Exclusivity Signal

Nothing on the page communicates that this is a private, selective club. The "Join" and "Bookings" nav links suggest anyone can walk in and pay. That kills the perceived value of membership.

New build: "Apply for Membership" language, founding spots counter, application screening
4

No Social Proof

128 people came to the launch event. Zero of those people are represented on the website. No testimonials, no event photos, no member count, no industry breakdown. All of that credibility is invisible.

New build: Testimonials section, event highlight, animated member counter
5

No Conversion Mechanism

The only call-to-action is "Contact us for group pricing" — language that sounds like renting a party room at a restaurant. There's no application form, no way to capture leads, no funnel. Every visitor who's interested hits a dead end.

New build: Multi-step application form with Supabase backend, instant team notifications
6

No Membership Tiers or Pricing

Visitors can't understand what they'd get or what it costs. Without clear tiers, there's no way to self-select or feel urgency around a particular level. The founding member opportunity — Paul's biggest lever — is completely hidden.

New build: Side-by-side Founding vs. Regular tier cards with pricing and benefits
7

No Cross-Business Story

Paul's biggest differentiator — access to deal flow across real estate, lending, wellness, wine, and tech — is nowhere on the page. This is the thing that makes Carpe Diem different from every other club, and it's completely absent.

New build: Benefits section with four pillars including cross-business networking
