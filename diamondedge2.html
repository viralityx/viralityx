60-Second Pre-Call Brief

Read this before you dial

Who he is: Robert runs a commercial general contracting and multifamily development firm out of Central Texas, with projects in TX, FL, NY, and CO (Pickleball Kingdom, Chamonix Condos, Algarita Apartments, Sbarro NYC, Babe Coffee Denver). He is Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned and SAM-verified. Rated 4.9 on Angi.

The way in: His SDVOSB certification is his most valuable and most underused asset. The private Austin multifamily pipeline is shrinking. Federal set-aside work is fenced off from his big competitors and he is already certified for it. If he is not working that channel, that is the headline of the proposal.

Do NOT pitch missed calls or $400 leads. He is not a home-services contractor. That framing kills your credibility in 30 seconds. Talk pipeline, bids, win rate, GovCon, and subcontractor bench.

Question Bank (open-ended, mapped to intel we need)
1. STORY & VISION
We need: where he is today, where he wants to go, what he cares about.
  • "Walk us through Diamond Edge. How'd you get started and where's the business today?"
  • "What are you most proud of building so far?"
  • "Where do you want this to be in two or three years?"Listen for: growth target, exit ambition, the work he wants more of.
2. BUSINESS MODEL & REVENUE MIX
We need: what kind of work, what size, what's actually profitable.
  • "Break it down for us. What's the split between commercial buildouts, multifamily and condo, and any government work?"
  • "What does an average project look like in size and dollars?"
  • "Which work do you actually want more of, and which just keeps the lights on?"Listen for: the high-margin lane to point the whole proposal at.
3. HOW HE WINS WORK (PIPELINE / BD) — CORE
We need: lead sources, bid volume, win rate, who owns BD, how pursuits are tracked.
  • "How does new work come to you today? Referrals, bid boards, repeat developers, relationships?"
  • "Roughly how many projects are you bidding or chasing in a given month?"
  • "When you bid, how often do you win? What's the difference between the ones you win and the ones you lose?"
  • "Who handles business development and estimating right now? Is that mostly you?"
  • "Walk me through what happens the moment a bid invite or lead comes in. Where does it live, how do you track it?"If the answer is "Excel" or "in my head" → CRM pipeline wedge, flag it.
  • "When a pursuit goes quiet, who owns the follow-up?"
4. GOVCON — THE ASSET
We need: are they working set-asides, who watches SAM.gov, past performance, the blocker.
  • "I saw you're Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned and SAM-verified. Are you actively going after federal or state set-aside work right now?"
  • "Who's watching SAM.gov for opportunities that fit you?"
  • "Do you have past performance on government jobs, or is that a lane you want to open?"
  • "What's stopped you from pushing harder on the set-aside side?"This is the wedge that makes the proposal. If he says "no time" or "don't know where to start" → GovCon monitoring agent is your headline.
5. DELIVERY & OPERATIONS
We need: where time and margin leak once a job is won.
  • "Once you win a job, what does running it look like? What are you using to manage schedule, budget, and the field?"
  • "Where do projects usually slip? Where does time or margin leak once you're building?"
  • "How much of the day-to-day still lives in your head or in spreadsheets?"
6. SUBCONTRACTORS
We need: how they source and qualify subs, bench depth, the pain.
  • "How do you find and qualify subs today?"
  • "In this labor market, how deep is your bench? Is sub availability ever what holds up a job?"
  • "When a sub applies through your site, what actually happens next?"We already know: nothing. Let him say it.
7. TEAM & ROBERT'S TIME
We need: org size, where the owner's time goes, what he hates doing.
  • "How big's the team and who does what?"
  • "Where does most of your week go? What are you doing that you wish you weren't?"
  • "If you had ten more hours a week, where would you put them?"
8. TECH STACK
We need: what they run now so the proposal integrates instead of replaces blindly.
  • "What software are you running for accounting, project management, estimating, CRM?"
  • "What do you like, and what do you fight with every day?"
9. REPUTATION & MARKETING
We need: how clients find them, review habits, how much presence matters to their buyers.
  • "How do most clients find you today?"
  • "Do you have any system for asking for reviews after a project wraps?"
  • "How much does your online presence matter to the kind of buyers and partners you want?"We know the 4.9 is invisible and socials are dead. Let him feel the gap.
The Close — Magic Wand

Save the most important question for last. Ask ONE. Then shut up.

"If you had a magic wand and could fix or automate one thing in the business tomorrow, what would it be?"
  • "What's the one thing that, if it worked the way you wanted, would change the game for Diamond Edge?"
  • "Of everything we covered, what do you need the most help with right now?"
Ask it, then go completely silent. Do not fill the gap. Whatever he says first is the headline of your proposal. Write it down word for word.

On The Call

DO

  • Open with one sharp observation about his business so he knows you did the work.
  • Ask, then go quiet. Count to three before you speak.
  • Mirror his words back: "So the bottleneck is really X?"
  • Take notes visibly. It signals this becomes something real.
  • Book the follow-up before you hang up.

DON'T

  • Pitch features or quote a price. This is discovery.
  • Mention missed calls or $400 leads. Wrong business.
  • Talk over him to fill silence.
  • Promise a specific dollar recovery yet. Gather first, model in the proposal.
  • Open more new verticals or ideas. Stay on his business.
Closing Script
"Robert, this is exactly what we needed. Here's what happens next. We take everything you just walked us through and build you a custom blueprint, mapped to Diamond Edge specifically, not a template. We'll lay out where the opportunity is, what it's worth, and what we'd build first. Let's get the next call on the calendar right now so I can walk you through it."
