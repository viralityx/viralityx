At Home Services (Nick)
Close Call Playbook

Goal of this call: walk the proposal, drop the numbers, and close. Josh says he's a done deal. Don't overtalk it, don't overwhelm him. Make it feel small, safe, and obvious.
60-Second Brief

Read before you dial

Where his head is: he's a one-man show drowning in admin, honest about it, and a little under-confident ("I don't know that I actually belong where I'm at"). He wants this. His only real wall is the money.

The whole call hinges on one number. He told Josh flat out: "$3,500, I just can't swing that." So the moment you show him $3,500 crossed out to $1,000, split in two, the objection is dead. Lead the money conversation with that, don't bury it.

His magic wand was crew, not marketing. "It'll be finding the help, you can't finish the quotes without the help." Open by connecting the plan to THAT, or he won't feel heard.

Do not oversell. He gets analysis paralysis. Fewer words, one clear next step, let Josh's 20 years of trust do the heavy lifting.

Objections & Answers
"I still don't know if I can swing it right now."
"That's why it's split. $500 today, $500 when it's live. And the $500 a month is less than one job's worth of the work you're already losing to voicemail every week. If it doesn't pay for itself, we'll fix it or you tell me to stop."
"What if I can't handle the extra work / I don't have the crew?"
"That's exactly why the hiring funnel is first. We're not going to flood you before you're ready. We build your bench first, then turn the leads up. You set the pace."
"I'm not really a tech guy."
"Good. That's the point. You don't touch any of it. You install windows, and it's simple enough that Vanessa can run it in a few minutes if she ever needs to."
"Most of my jobs come from Pella anyway."
"And they stay 100% yours. We never touch those. The 10% only applies to work we bring you that you wouldn't have had. This is about stopping what's leaking and adding on top."
"Let me think about it."
Josh: "Brother, you've been thinking about it. What's the actual hang-up?" Then answer THAT. He's an action-taker with analysis paralysis, he needs a nudge, not more information.
Before You Hang Up

Lock these three, live

  • Start date. "We start Monday." Get it on the calendar.
  • The first $500. Get the payment method set on the call. Don't let it become an invoice you chase.
  • Access. Website, Google Business Profile, and his brother's contact (he built the site, we'll need him). Grab it live, not as homework, he'll forget for two days, he told us so himself.
Watch-Outs

Handle with care

  • Don't flood him. He's solo with unreliable crew. Leads before capacity hurts his brand and our reputation. Crew funnel first, then volume.
  • Don't mention the Ford plant. He corrected us on that, it fizzled. Bringing it up says we weren't listening.
  • Don't pitch the podcast play hard yet. Danny and Josh agreed: prove Nick's results first, then work the network. Mention the $500 referral as HIS win, then leave it.
  • He's under-confident about the money side. Reassure, don't push. "You belong here, you've got a Pella contract most guys can't get."
  • Delivery has to be real. He's Josh's 20-year friend and the doorway to a whole town. If we fumble Nick, we lose the town and Josh's credibility with it.
