The context

The timing is the whole story

A boom on your doorstep

The BlueOval SK battery plant in Glendale is bringing thousands of jobs and a wave of new homes and remodels to Hardin County. Windows, doors, and paint demand is surging. Whoever captures the search and referral flow owns the next few years.

Big-ticket means follow-up wins

A window or door job is a considered, financed purchase. Most are won in the follow-up, not the first call. Without a nurture, quoted jobs quietly go cold.

In home services, speed wins

The first credible response usually books the job. When you are on a jobsite and a call or quote request comes in, minutes decide who gets the work.

Your proof sells the job

Homeowners choose by before-and-after photos and reviews. Right now your gallery reads "no images available" and your reviews are nowhere on the site.

Where it leaks

Five gaps between you and the boom

1. Leads wait while you're on a jobsite

Your only ways in are a call, a text, and a quote form. When you are up a ladder, that call goes unanswered and that form sits until you get to it. In home improvement, the homeowner is calling three companies, and the one who answers first usually wins the job.

2. Quotes go cold with no follow-up

A homeowner gets a window quote, then thinks about it for weeks, often around financing. With no automated follow-up reminding them of the value, the warranty, and the payment options, a real share of quoted jobs simply drift to whoever stayed in touch.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $3,000
Assumption: a portion of quoted-but-unclosed big-ticket jobs each month are recoverable with a structured follow-up and financing nurture. Estimate pending validation.

When a new Hardin County homeowner searches "window replacement Elizabethtown" or "Pella dealer near me," that is a ready-to-buy customer. If you are not ranking and your Google presence is not dialed, that high-intent demand, the demand the plant is creating, goes to a competitor.

5. Your network isn't a referral engine yet
