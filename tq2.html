What You Have Already Built
Most audits open by telling a business what is broken. Yours starts by naming what is not, because it changes where the real problem is.

A real product, not a landing page

AI dispatch with HOS-aware matching, ELD sync, document OCR, a driver app, settlements and IFTA reporting. This is a full logistics OS at v4.2.1, not a thin wrapper. The product is not the bottleneck.

Clean, self-serve pricing

$45 and $40 per truck per month, Stripe billing, a 15-day unrestricted trial, no contracts. Low friction to say yes. That is a strength, and it is exactly why the funnel around it matters so much.

A dual motion already in place

Small fleets self-serve through the trial. Custom and enterprise route to sales. You have the right structure. What is missing is the machinery that works each path automatically.

Enterprise-grade positioning

Amazon Freight, Samsara, Motive, DAT, and JB Hunt sit on your homepage as the world you plug into. You are asking fleets to switch systems. That is a high-consideration decision, and high-consideration decisions are won on speed and follow-up.

Where The Gap Is

You automated the fleet. The customer acquisition around it still looks manual. Every button on the site funnels a fleet operator into one of two places, a free trial or a Contact Sales form, and from the outside there is no sign of what happens in the minutes and days after. In SaaS, that gap between a signup and a human, and between a trial start and the first real use, is where most of the revenue quietly leaks. A TMS is a considered purchase made by busy dispatchers and owner-operators. If nobody reaches them fast, and nothing pulls a stalled trial back, the product never gets the chance to prove itself. The five items below are the specific points where that happens.

Market Context

Speed decides who wins the switch

Contact a lead within 5 minutes and you are far more likely to convert than a competitor who takes an hour. In a crowded TMS market, the fleet is usually evaluating two or three systems at once. First real response tends to win the trial.

Free trials live or die on activation

Self-serve SaaS trials commonly convert in the 15 to 25% range when the user actually reaches first value. Unactivated trials convert in the low single digits. For a complex product like a TMS, guided onboarding is the difference, not the free days.

The market is switching in your favor

Fleets are moving off spreadsheets and legacy TMS toward modern platforms. That tailwind means more trials and inquiries over time, which makes every point of funnel leakage more expensive, not less.

Your ACV rewards fixing this once

A 10-truck fleet is ~$4,800/yr, a 25-truck fleet is ~$12,000/yr, and both stick for years on a system of record. Recovering even a handful of trials and enterprise conversations a month compounds into serious ARR.

Bottlenecks
Illustrative model assumes roughly 75 self-serve trial starts and 15 sales-led inquiries per month. Your real numbers replace these on the call.

1. Speed-to-lead on Contact Sales and enterprise inquiries

Your biggest deals, Custom and Enterprise, all arrive through a Contact Sales form. A multi-truck fleet filling that out is actively shopping systems and almost always talking to other vendors. There is no visible instant response, no automated qualification, no booked next step. If a human does not reach them inside minutes, the warmest moment is gone and a competitor gets the demo.

ESTIMATED NEW MRR AT RISK: ~$1,400/mo
Assumption: ~15 sales-led inquiries/mo, ~3 to 4 lost to slow or no response, ~2 recoverable at an estimated ~$700/mo each. Modeled as new MRR not added per month. Estimate pending your real inquiry volume and current response time.

2. Free trials that start and never activate

The 15-day trial is your main growth engine, and it is self-serve. A TMS has real setup, adding trucks, syncing ELD, getting drivers onto the app, running a first dispatch. A user who signs up and does not reach that first real use almost never converts, and today nothing appears to guide them there or notice when they stall. Most of the trial revenue leaks here, silently, before anyone talks to them.

ESTIMATED NEW MRR AT RISK: ~$2,100/mo
Assumption: ~75 trials/mo. Unmanaged conversion ~8% versus an achievable ~18% with activation support, a gap of ~7 to 8 customers/mo at an estimated ~$280/mo average. Benchmarks, not your figures, until validated.

3. No qualification or routing between self-serve and sales

A 40-truck operation may click Register and land in self-serve when it should be a sales-led enterprise deal. A single owner-operator may hit Contact Sales and consume rep time that will never pay back. Without inbound scoring by fleet size and role, both motions run at reduced conversion and your team spends time in the wrong places.

ESTIMATED NEW MRR AT RISK: ~$800/mo
Assumption: mis-routing depresses conversion on both paths and wastes rep capacity. Modeled conservatively at ~$800/mo in delayed and lost new MRR.

4. No reactivation on abandoned trials and demo no-shows

People start the demo flow and drop off. Trials expire without a decision. Booked demos no-show. In trucking, that is not disinterest, it is a dispatcher who got slammed. Right now there is no automated win-back reaching those people across voice, text, and email to pull them back into the funnel. Every one of them was already interested enough to raise a hand once.

ESTIMATED NEW MRR AT RISK: ~$850/mo
Assumption: ~3 recoverable per month across abandoned trials and no-shows at ~$280/mo average. Estimate pending your drop-off and no-show rates.

5. Thin proof and top-of-funnel

About, Testimonials, News, and Blog read as placeholder or early. For a fleet being asked to move its system of record, social proof and content are not decoration, they are conversion. Weak proof lowers trial-to-paid and raises acquisition cost on every channel you run. This is the one gap that quietly taxes all four above.

ESTIMATED NEW MRR AT RISK: ~$650/mo unrealized
Assumption: thin proof drags conversion across the funnel. Modeled as ~$650/mo of new MRR not captured, ramping as content and proof build.
