The context

Demand is not your problem. Capture is.

The patients are out there

Over 70 million Americans have sleep problems, and most of them research at 2am, when they cannot sleep. The demand is enormous. The question is only whether they can reach you.

You are reachable 18 hours a week

Open Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, 9:30 to 3:30. Every other hour, a new patient or referral who calls hits a wall, and in this field they simply call the next clinic.

Sleep medicine is a recall business

CPAP adherence, resupply, annual follow-ups. A large share of your reimbursable revenue lives in the follow-up, and nothing automated is running it today.

Patients vet you online first

Before booking, they check your reviews and your website. Your reputation is excellent. Your digital presence is quietly working against it.

Where it leaks

Five gaps costing you patients and revenue

1. Limited hours, and no way to capture anyone outside them

You are open three days a week for six hours. There is no after-hours line, no chat, no text capture. A patient with insomnia searching at night, or a primary-care office referring on a Friday, reaches voicemail or nothing. In sleep medicine, that patient does not wait. They book elsewhere.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $5,000
Assumption: roughly 4 to 5 new-patient inquiries per month lost to the hours you are closed, at an estimated first-year value of $1,000+ each (consult, sleep study, CPAP setup, and follow-ups, insurance reimbursed). Estimate pending your actuals.

2. The website is undermining a doctor patients already trust

Your live site still carries placeholder Latin text, a "Services" section showing finance-consulting and marketing template blocks instead of your sleep services, dead social icons, a public gmail address, and buttons that point to leftover staging URLs. A prospective patient checking your credibility sees a site that does not match the caliber of care you give.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $3,000
Assumption: a meaningful share of prospective patients who reach the site leave without booking because the presentation reads as unfinished or untrustworthy. Estimate pending validation.

3. Booking is fragmented and intake is manual

There are several different booking paths (a contact form, a healow link, old staging pages) and no single clean way to self-schedule. New-patient paperwork is a PDF packet the patient prints and fills by hand. That is friction at the exact moment a patient is ready to commit, and it burns your front-desk time.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $2,500
Assumption: booking friction and manual intake delay or lose new patients and consume staff hours that could go to care. Estimate pending validation.

4. No recall or CPAP-adherence engine

Sleep patients are lifelong: CPAP compliance monitoring (which insurers require), resupply, annual studies, and the patients who quietly fall off therapy. With no automated recall, lapsed patients stay lapsed, and reimbursable follow-up visits never get booked.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $2,500
Assumption: lapsed CPAP and follow-up patients not re-engaged. Each reactivation is a reimbursable visit and often ongoing management. Estimate pending validation.

5. A 5-star reputation that isn't working for you

Your testimonials are genuinely strong, patients who followed you across practices for years. But that proof is not surfaced where new patients and referring physicians look. For a solo practice competing with large suburban groups, reviews are how you get found and trusted.

ESTIMATED MONTHLY LEAK: $1,000
Assumption: under-collected and unsurfaced reviews suppress new-patient discovery and referral confidence. Estimate pending validation.
