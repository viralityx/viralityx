Section 03 · Master This

The Marketing Director Two-Step

Selling to a non-owner contact is a two-stage motion. If you treat the marketing director like the buyer, you will burn the lead. If you treat them like an internal champion you are equipping to sell up to ownership, you win.

Step One · Sell the marketing director on what they control

The layers THEY own

Customer database (CDP), email/SMS marketing automation, review engine, social content operations, catering inquiry nurture, loyalty program design. These are operational systems the marketing director can champion and largely run without ownership signing off on every detail. Lead with these in the call.

The hook

"I want to give you a system that makes your existing work compound and gets you to weekly reporting Brian and Matt actually look forward to seeing."

Step Two · Position the agency as something they champion UP

The layers OWNERSHIP controls

POS integration, staff scheduling, kitchen ops, inventory, game-day operations, catering pricing strategy, third-party delivery contract decisions. These need Brian + Matt's sign-off. Frame these as "the bigger conversation we'd want to have with the partners on a follow-up call."

The ask

"Want me to put together a 2-page summary of what we'd build on the marketing-ops side AND a separate one-pager for Brian and Matt on the portfolio-wide infrastructure? Two docs, two audiences, you decide who sees what."

Why this works

The marketing director gets an immediate win for their own job. They get permission and language to bring the bigger conversation to ownership without it feeling like a sales pitch. You make them the hero of the internal conversation, not the messenger of a vendor pitch.

  • 45% of diners changed favorite restaurant in the past year. Up from 33% in 2025. Restaurant loyalty is the most volatile it has been in a decade. Loyalty members visit 22% more often, spend 38% more per visit. Independents that own the loyalty layer first in their market own it permanently. For The Table has 12+ years of regulars they currently can't recognize systematically.
  • Third-party delivery is 35 to 45% real effective cost. Headline 25 to 30%, real total once packaging, promos, processing, refunds layer in. Home weekends produce a meaningful chunk of the portfolio's annual revenue. Manual game-day ops leak 10 to 20% of capacity. AI-driven waitlist + phone overflow recovers it.
  • Multi-brand CDPs lift cross-brand revenue 20 to 35% inside 12 months. Industry data. A Charlie Park brunch regular who throws a 50-person rehearsal dinner is invisible to Social Catering until they fill out a fresh inquiry form. Cross-brand recognition is the highest single revenue lever in the entire operation.
  • Catering inquiry ghosting is 60 to 80%. Long-cycle, high-ticket sales motion. Most groups never build the structured nurture sequence that recovers ghosted inquiries. Recovering even 15% on a $10K average ticket is $30K to $60K per quarter. Single highest-ROI deployment in the stack.

Objection prep (rehearse these once)

"I'd need to check with ownership / Brian / Matt before we do anything."

You: 100% expected. That's exactly why I'm coming to you first instead of cold-calling them. The 20-minute Blueprint Review with Daniel is for YOU. You see the system, you stress-test it, you decide if it's worth bringing to Brian and Matt. If it is, we put together a separate one-pager tuned specifically to the portfolio-wide pieces they'd want to weigh in on. If you decide it isn't, no awkward "I have to break it to ownership" conversation. You're in control of the next step.

"We already have a marketing team / I'm the marketing person."

You: Good, that's actually what makes this work. We're not a marketing agency, we don't replace what you do. What we do is build the systems behind what you do so your existing work compounds instead of starting from zero every week. Right now I'd bet you're spending 60% of your time on reactive stuff (responding to reviews, updating 5 Instagrams, chasing catering inquiries, fielding game-day calls) and 40% on the strategic work you actually want to do. We flip that ratio.

"We have a POS / email tool / system already, do you replace it?"

You: We sit on top of what you have. Whatever you're using for POS, reservations, email, Lendr-equivalents, we integrate. We're the connective tissue, not a replacement. The whole point is the CDP layer that consolidates everything you've already built into one customer view. That's also why Daniel walking through your specific stack on the Blueprint Review matters, he'll show you exactly which integrations we'd light up and which we'd leave alone.

"This sounds expensive. What does it cost?"

You: Depends on what the portfolio actually needs, and a 5-brand group is a different conversation than a single concept. That's why the Blueprint Review with Daniel is the right next step. He'll walk you through what we'd build, in what order, and you'll get a real number against the real situation. Pricing without context just sounds like a number you'd argue with. Also, if cost ends up being the question for ownership, we can structure phased deployments so the first phase pays for the next.

"Just send me the audit and I'll take a look."

You: Happy to. I'll send it right now. The audit is sharp on its own but the real value is the 20 minutes where Daniel walks through your specific numbers live, especially the cross-brand customer database piece and the catering inquiry nurture, those are nuanced and easier to see than read. After you've read it, want me to lock you in for one of his slots this week? Even a tentative hold. If it lands flat after you read, easy to cancel.
