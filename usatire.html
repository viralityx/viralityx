Section 01 · Discovery Reframe

What Changed After The Conversation

The pre-discovery audit was built from the outside-in. The May 19 call corrected several core assumptions. Before we scope anything, we want this on the record — so the roadmap below is anchored on what's actually true, not what we guessed from the website.

Reframe 01
Business model is 100% B2B wholesale. The audit assumed a B2B + B2C retail hybrid and called out conflicting audiences on the homepage. Correction: retail isn't an option for USA Wheel — it would jeopardize the distributor relationships that make the business work. That kills two of the audit's recommendations (B2C funnel split, retail review engine framing) and sharpens everything else around the wholesale dealer journey.
Reframe 02
GHL + Twilio is already deployed. Has been for roughly a year. Pipelines are built, SMS and email sequences have been run — and the results have been disappointing: roughly 10 replies per week from thousands of sends. Musa was candid: this year would have been the last on GHL if nothing changed. Correction: this is not a new-software sale. This is a "operators who can actually run the system you already pay for" sale. ViralityX runs the same stack. Tuning beats replacing.
Reframe 03
Call volume is 200–300/day with a 4–5% miss rate during business hours. Most missed calls do call back (~90%). After-hours volume isn't dead — but the framing has to shift: B2B customers can't take delivery overnight, so the after-hours value isn't "close the sale," it's "capture intent before they call the competitor in the morning." Voice agent gives a quote, holds the lead, books the morning call.
Reframe 04
The real pain is sales, not lead capture. Magic-wand answer on the call: "Sales. Another two, three million in the door." Lead source today is word of mouth, outside sales team, and referrals — zero SEO, PPC, or social. Employee retention is the deeper problem: experienced tire-industry reps are hard to find and harder to keep in South Atlanta. Every system in this roadmap is built to answer one question: how do we get more deals through the door without depending on hiring more reps?
Reframe 05
Three locations operate independently by design. Three warehouses, three teams, three email identities — not a mistake, a structure. Correction: we don't centralize what shouldn't be centralized. We build a unified visibility layer on top — one CRM view, location-routed workflows, aggregated KPI reporting — without disrupting how each warehouse runs day-to-day.
Reframe 06
Review ceiling is naturally lower than the audit projected. Atlanta services ~200 tire shops total — so a review engine can't generate thousands of reviews from nothing. The right target is 200+ Google reviews from the existing customer base in 90 days, with sustained capture on every transaction going forward. That's enough to dominate Google Maps for "wheel and tire wholesaler Atlanta" without overstating what the market can produce.
Section 02 · Updated Diagnosis

What's Actually Broken

Six bottlenecks that surfaced in the call. Each is the direct source of a specific revenue gap — and each is something a properly-configured operations team can close inside 90 days. Ranked by impact on Musa's stated goal: more deals, faster.

01
GHL is running but not operating
Thousands of outbound SMS and emails sent. Roughly 10 replies per week. That's not a software problem — it's the absence of an operator who reads the data, tunes the sequence, and rebuilds the funnel based on what's actually converting. GHL can do an enormous amount; you're using a fraction of it. This is the single most addressable gap in the business right now.
Severity · Critical
02
No replacement layer for the experienced sales rep you can't hire
Competitors in Atlanta South have one structural advantage that's hard to copy: senior tire-industry sales reps with deep customer relationships. You've tried to hire them — train cycle is 3 months, retention is 6–9 months, then they leave. A 24/7 AI voice agent trained on your product catalog, pricing logic, and objection handling does not solve the relationship problem — but it absorbs the inbound and outbound call load that you currently lose to "we couldn't pick up" or "the new rep didn't know the answer."
Severity · Critical
03
No after-hours capture for the 73 hours of unmanned phones
B2B closes at 5pm — agreed. But during those 73 weekly hours, shops still call to scope pricing, check availability, and decide who they're going to order from in the morning. Right now those calls go to voicemail or hang up. A voice agent that captures the size, the shop name, the quantity needed, and books a callback for the morning turns the dead window into a queue of first-thing-tomorrow orders.
Severity · High
04
Google Business Profile underleveraged
Birdeye wasn't even on Musa's radar — Google reviews are the actual lever. The Amazon-card experiment proved review velocity moves when sales reps push it, then drops the moment they stop. Automated post-transaction SMS (low/no-cost, persistent, every customer) keeps the lift running without depending on rep behavior. Target: 200+ Google reviews from the existing dealer base inside 90 days.
Severity · High
05
No KPI dashboard, no weekly visibility into what's actually working
Musa's instinct is right: "we need somebody on top of the operation." There's data inside QuickBooks, the custom WMS, and GHL — but no one weekly cuts it into which shops are growing, which are dormant, which sales reps are converting, which SKUs are moving. Without that view, every decision (which list to call, which inventory to push, which location to staff up) is intuition, not signal.
Severity · High
06
Outbound list activation is manual and one-way
The current outbound motion: scrape Google for tire shops by ZIP, hand the list to sales reps, cold call. Reps burn out, list goes stale, no closed-loop tracking on which shops were contacted, which responded, which converted. An AI-driven outbound layer can keep the list fresh weekly, send targeted opener sequences, route warm replies to a human rep, and feed everything back into the CRM with attribution.
Severity · Medium
