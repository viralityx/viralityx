Industry Context

Why the Investor-Side Window Is Wide Open

Reg D 506(c) raises sit at a unique inflection point in 2026. The infrastructure to run them well exists, the regulatory environment just got easier, and most regional fund sponsors are still raising the same way they did in 2018.

The regulatory shift: The SEC's March 2025 no-action letter and follow-up staff guidance materially simplified accredited investor verification under Rule 506(c). Sponsors can now rely on minimum investment thresholds plus written representations rather than full document review for most investors. Translation: the friction at the bottom of the funnel just dropped. The bottleneck moved upstream, into the nurture window between "submitted form" and "wired capital."


The funnel reality: Across private real estate debt funds, the median accredited investor decision cycle runs 60 to 180 days from first interest to first wire. Most regional sponsors lose 70 to 85% of opted-in leads in that gap, simply because there is no sequenced education running. The investor's question is never "is 10% interesting?" The question is "do I trust you enough to send $100K?" That trust is built in the 6 to 12 touches between the opt-in and the call, not on the landing page.


The competitive lens: Operators like PeerStreet, Groundfloor, Fund That Flip, and the Yieldi accredited investor channel run sophisticated multi-stage nurture (monthly performance reports, retargeting pixels, narrated explainer libraries, automated DRIP onboarding, accredited verification baked into the form). Regional sponsors who are raising in-person, by word-of-mouth, or off a single landing page are competing against that infrastructure whether they realize it or not. Every accredited investor evaluating a $100K placement is comparing the experience.

This is what happens when an accredited investor clicks on a Monument City Capital ad, podcast mention, or referral link today. The steps marked in red are where the journey loses people.

5

Manual follow-up window (unknown duration)

Visible gap: No public visibility into response SLA. Industry data on capital-raise funnels: leads that don't get a personalized response within 24 hours drop to under 12% conversion. With Shaun running both borrower and investor operations off the same shared phone line, this window almost certainly stretches.

6

Visible gap: Whether scheduled or recovered, this step depends entirely on the prospect's persistence and Shaun's calendar bandwidth. There is no automated booking, no pre-call qualification questionnaire, no pre-call materials sent to compress call time, no automated reminder sequence.

For LPs writing $250K+ checks, the post-commitment experience is the single biggest driver of upsizing and referral.

Capital-Raise Bottlenecks

Where Investor Trust Is Leaking

Each bottleneck below was identified through a direct review of investwithmcc.com (the standard URL and the /opt2-365689 variant), benchmarked against the standard top-quartile Reg D 506(c) sponsors are running in 2026. The investor funnel does many things right at the top. The breakage is downstream.

01

Placeholder Testimonials Are Live on the Page

The single biggest credibility hit visible right now. The investor testimonial section displays the literal text "Placeholder testimonials, awaiting real investor quotes" above three sample quotes from "James R., Retired CFO," "Dr. David K., Physician," and "Michael T., Business Owner." For an accredited investor evaluating a $100K placement, that exact label reads as "they don't yet have investor testimonials they can publish." That is not what an accredited LP wants to see at the moment they're weighing trust. Top sponsors publish real, photographed, attributable investor quotes with full bios, often with video. The gap between placeholder and real isn't a copywriting issue, it's the entire trust transfer mechanism. Until those are live and credible, the landing page is doing less than half the persuasion work it could be.

Severity: Critical
02

No Visible Post-Opt-In Nurture Sequence

The end-state of the funnel is a confirmation screen that says "Check your email for the Investor Overview and Business Plan. Shaun or a member of the team will follow up shortly." That sentence is the entire post-opt-in mechanism visible from the outside. For a Reg D 506(c) raise where the typical decision cycle is 60 to 180 days, that means 8 to 24 weeks of weekly touches that should be happening automatically, are not visibly happening at all. No monthly fund performance summary, no Shaun-narrated short videos delivered over time, no market commentary, no investor education series, no "what your first month would look like" walkthrough, no retargeting on the user's pixel. 80% of accredited investors who would have converted on a 90-day timeline disengage in week 3 to 4 if the nurture sequence is empty. Right now it appears to be empty.

Severity: Critical
03

Accreditation Qualifier Is a Dead-End UI Element

The funnel has a self-attestation Accreditation Qualifier that returns "You May Qualify" or "You May Not Qualify." That is good UX. But it appears to run entirely on the client side, with no CRM-side scoring, no segmentation of leads who qualified vs didn't, no automated routing of qualified leads into a fast-track sequence, no triggered hand-off of unqualified leads into a "stay on our list for when your situation changes" path. Self-attestation is the easy part. Real accreditation verification through VerifyInvestor, Parallel Markets, EarlyIQ, or InvestReady is the part Reg D 506(c) sponsors at scale automate. Without it, every investor commitment requires Shaun's team to handle accreditation evidence by email, which compounds time-to-wire on a deal-by-deal basis.

Severity: Critical
04

No Investor Portal for Active LPs

After an LP wires capital, the funnel goes dark from a public visibility standpoint. There is no LP login on the public domain, no monthly statement portal, no embedded performance dashboard, no document vault for the PPM, subscription agreements, K-1s, or distribution statements. Accredited LPs writing $100K to $1M checks expect the same caliber of post-commitment experience they see from iCapital, CAIS, AppFolio Investment Manager, or InvestNext. Without that, every distribution period creates a manual operations burden on Shaun's team, and every K-1 season creates 50+ individualized emails. The post-wire experience is also the highest-leverage upsize and referral driver. An LP who feels like an institutional investor placements $250K next year instead of holding at $100K.

Severity: High
05

Founder Authority Underused on the Investor Side

Shaun has a real authority surface: years coaching at a top national REI education company, multiple podcast appearances, keynote speaking experience. On the investor funnel, the only use of that authority is a static "Meet Shaun Magner" section, a 3-minute overview video, and one mid-page pull quote. For accredited investors weighing months of due diligence, the Shaun-narrated content should be doing far more structural work. A monthly investor letter from Shaun, podcast-style audio summaries of each month's lending activity, a "Q&A with the Founder" segment, an embedded library of his prior podcast appearances on real estate strategy. His credibility is the single most underleveraged asset in the entire investor funnel. Right now it sits as one page section instead of running as a content engine.

Severity: High
06

Single Shared Phone Line Across Borrowers and Investors

The investor landing page lists 443-750-1565, the same line that serves the borrower funnel. A flipper calling about a 72-hour close and an accredited physician calling about a $250K placement both ring the same phone. Beyond the routing issue the first audit raised, there's a deeper problem on the investor side. Accredited investors calling about a six-figure placement will draw inferences about fund operations from how the phone gets answered. If they call during business hours and get voicemail because Shaun is on a borrower call, the implicit signal is "this is a small operation that I would be a meaningful percentage of." That's not the impression a $100K minimum, $25M-history fund wants to leave with a first-time prospect.

Severity: High
07

Referral Program Has No Activation Infrastructure

The funnel mentions a 1% referral program: "Earn 1% of their initial investment through our referral program, or choose to donate your referral fee to a charity of your choice." That is a smart, low-cost growth lever. But it's a sentence on a static page. There is no visible referral-link generator for existing LPs, no automated tracking of who referred whom, no payout automation, no "share with a colleague" CTA in the post-wire experience, no LP-facing dashboard showing referral status. For a fund whose primary growth channel SHOULD be word-of-mouth between accredited investors, leaving the referral program manual is leaving the multiplier on the table. An LP who places $250K and has 4 accredited friends in their network is the cheapest capital acquisition path in the entire business.

Severity: High
08

Third-Party Funnel Host Limits Stack Continuity

The funnel runs on automatethejourney.com infrastructure rather than on monumentcitycapital.com directly. That's a normal pattern for paid-traffic landing pages, but it raises questions about data continuity. What happens when an investor opts in on the variant URL? Where does that lead live? Is it piped into a CRM that also sees the borrower-side data? Are retargeting pixels firing across both domains? Is Shaun's team able to see the full marketing-to-distribution lifecycle in one place, or is it scattered across the third-party builder, an email tool, a spreadsheet, and the fund administrator's portal? The structural risk is that the marketing layer and the operations layer don't talk to each other, which means follow-up sequences depend on someone manually exporting CSVs.

Severity: Medium
09

No Visible Investor Comfort on Compliance Posture

Reg D 506(c) raises require the sponsor to take "reasonable steps to verify" accredited status. The funnel mentions the PPM and offering documents in disclosures, which is appropriate, but the front-end communicates almost nothing about the compliance and operational rigor behind the scenes. Sophisticated LPs want to see references to the fund administrator, the auditor, the legal counsel, the third-party servicer for distributions, the AML/KYC partner, the accreditation verification partner. For LPs comparing this fund against an iCapital or PeerStreet, the absence of those signals reads as "small shop without institutional plumbing." Whether or not that's true matters less than what the prospect perceives.

Severity: Medium
