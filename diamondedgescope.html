Section 2

Where leads and opportunities come from

New work is generated and captured through the systems and channels ViralityX builds and runs, and separately, government opportunities are surfaced by the set-aside monitor. Sources include:

  • Government: SAM.gov and other public bid sources, filtered to your NAICS codes, certifications, place of performance, and bond capacity.
  • Inbound: your website, Google presence, and campaigns we run.
  • Outbound & referral: targeted outreach and any referral or affiliate channels we set up.

Your own existing relationships, referrals, and any opportunity you or your team already had in hand are not ViralityX-sourced (see Section 9).

Section 3

How leads are qualified and verified

Every lead runs through the same path before it reaches your team, so nobody chases unqualified work:

  • Capture: the lead lands in one place with its source recorded automatically.
  • Qualify: a questionnaire and screening step scores the lead for fit (project type, scope, timeline, budget range, service area) before it moves forward.
  • Verify: qualified leads are confirmed as real and in-scope; unqualified ones are filtered out or nurtured, not handed to your team.
  • Route: the qualified lead is assigned to the right person by business line, with its source, status, and next step attached.

ViralityX owns capture, qualification, verification, routing, and follow-up. Your team owns the human sales contact, estimating, and the proposal (see Section 6).

Section 4

Section 7

How subcontractors are sourced, vetted, and approved

We build on your process and tighten it. Every subcontractor is captured and gated on the same requirements before they can be used on a project:

  • Licenses, insurance certificates, and workers comp
  • W-9 on file
  • Trade categories and service areas
  • References and pricing
  • Explicit approval by Diamond Edge before assignment to any job

Approved subs become an organized, searchable bench so you can staff a job quickly with vetted people only.

Section 8

What Diamond Edge owns

Yours: all of your data, your leads and contacts, your customer and subcontractor records, your accounts (website, Google, Contractor Foreman), and the reports we produce for you. If we ever part ways, this stays with you.

Ours: ViralityX retains its systems, tools, methods, workflows, and intellectual property. The build stays on our side; the data and results are yours.

Section 9

What counts as a ViralityX-sourced job

A job counts as ViralityX-sourced only if the lead was generated and qualified through ViralityX systems, with a tracked source record to prove it. That record is the proof.

  • Government: an opportunity counts only if it was surfaced, matched, and qualified by our set-aside monitor, not one you or Dana already had.
  • Excluded, always: your existing referrals, relationships, repeat customers, and any public bid you would have found on your own that did not come through our qualification.

If a job's source is ever unclear, the tracked record decides it. No record, no fee.

Section 10

How the performance fee is calculated and paid

ViralityX earns 15% of the net job profit on each ViralityX-sourced job.

Net job profit means the contract value minus the direct costs of that job: labor, materials, subcontractors, equipment and rentals, permitting, and project supervision assigned to the job. General company overhead, executive compensation, and costs not specific to the job are not deducted.

When it's due: the fee is calculated on final job profit and is due as Diamond Edge collects payment, so retainage and unpaid balances are only counted once they are actually received. During the initial period, both sides keep transparent job-cost records so the math is clear and agreed.

To confirm together

This is the proposed basis, designed to fairly account for your job-specific costs while keeping the calculation clean and verifiable. If you'd like to adjust the deductible costs or the structure, this is the one item we'll finalize together before signing.

Section 11

Weekly reporting and KPIs

We review a shared report together each week, covering:

  • Leads by source and division, and pipeline stage movement
  • Response times and follow-up activity
  • Bids, win rate, and jobs won
  • ViralityX-sourced job tracker (for fee transparency)
  • Government opportunities surfaced and pursued
  • Reviews collected and presence metrics
