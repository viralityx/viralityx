ICP Fit Note

A Different Operational Shape Than the Typical ViralityX Client

Most ViralityX clients are local service businesses with phones that ring, technicians in the field, and a CRM that needs to route those calls. Purovitalis is a DTC supplement brand with a global subscription base and a companion biomarker app. The mechanics are different, but the operational AI layer is just as critical, the leaks live in different places.

Where the AI ops thesis still applies, even on a DTC brand:


1. Subscription customer service is a service business. Every "where's my order," "can I take this with my blood-pressure meds," "I want to pause," and "how does NMN interact with X" is a support ticket. Industry data: AI phone + chat support resolves 35 to 45% of supplement support contacts within the first 90 days of deployment. BioLongevity Labs handles 79% of inbound calls fully autonomously. The economics here are not theoretical, they are happening now.


2. Subscription churn is the entire business. Supplement DTC averages 5 to 8% monthly churn on monthly billing, with first-month churn running 20 to 30%. Top-quartile brands have moved from 36% to 55%+ annual retention through structured save-flows, pause logic, and biomarker-tied re-engagement. Every percentage point of retention compounds into 7-figure LTV swing at Purovitalis scale.


3. The Aura biomarker app is a service layer. 50+ biomarkers, AI insight delivery, smartphone screening. That's a clinical-adjacent workflow with data privacy, repeat engagement, and protocol-recommendation logic baked in. It is a service business inside the e-commerce shell.

Industry Context

The Longevity Wave Purovitalis Is Riding

Before diagnosing the operation, the macro. Longevity supplements moved from fringe to mainstream between 2021 and 2026, and the brands that own the next 36 months will be the ones that solved retention and clinical authority, not the ones that won acquisition.

The category: Longevity and healthy-aging supplements sit inside a global wellness market projected past $60B. NMN, NAD+, resveratrol, spermidine, and quercetin have all crossed into mainstream awareness, driven by Sinclair, Attia, Huberman, and the broader longevity media cycle. Purovitalis sells in this exact bucket with a European clean-label moat.


The operational challenge: Customer acquisition cost in supplement DTC has roughly doubled since 2022. The brands winning today are not acquiring cheaper, they are retaining longer and selling deeper into existing subscribers. Annual retention has improved category-wide from 36% to 40%, top quartile sits at 55%+. The brands at 55%+ all have three things in common: structured pause-and-skip save flows, AI-driven customer service that handles 35%+ of contacts, and a clinical authority signal (founder, lab partner, or biomarker tie-in) that justifies premium pricing.


Purovitalis is positioned to take the top quartile. The Aura app, the liposomal delivery story, the COA transparency, and the EU manufacturing story are all premium-pricing moats already built. The audit below assesses where the operational layer is leaving margin on the table.

A customer who takes the quiz, downloads the Aura app, and subscribes to NMN should look like one identity. From the public site, they appear to live in three different systems.

Surface 01 · DTC E-Commerce

Revenue Bottlenecks

Where the Margin Is Leaking

Each bottleneck below was identified through a live audit of purovitalis.com, the Aura app store listings, the Trustpilot review surface, the founder's positioning content, competitor longevity brands (Tru Niagen, Donotage, Renue By Science, NOVOS, Timeline), and 2026 DTC supplement subscription benchmarks. These are gaps measurable against the top-quartile longevity DTC operator in the 2026 to 2027 market.

01

No Visible AI Customer Service Layer Across Subscription Lifecycle

A longevity supplement brand at this scale generates a predictable, high-volume stream of support contacts: "where's my order," "can I take this with my statin," "I want to pause this month," "what's the difference between liposomal NMN and the powder version," "do I take this with food." There is no visible AI chat agent, AI phone agent, or smart-help-center deployment on purovitalis.com beyond standard FAQ pages. Industry benchmark: AI phone + chat handles 35 to 45% of supplement support volume within 90 days of deployment. BioLongevity Labs resolves 79% of calls fully autonomously. For a brand with 412+ Trustpilot reviews and likely 5-figure monthly contact volume, this is the largest single operational cost still being paid in human labor.

Severity: Critical
02

Subscription Save-Flow + Pause Logic Likely Underbuilt

The "Start Plans" framing is sharp (3 months is the right consistency window for NMN to show effect). What is not visible from the outside is the save-flow that fires when a customer hits cancel. Top-quartile DTC supplement brands run a structured cascade: offer pause, offer skip-a-month, offer downsize SKU, offer a free Aura re-scan to show progress, offer a one-time discount, then cancel cleanly. Each step preserves 6 to 12 months of LTV. Industry data: pause-and-skip save flows preserve up to 12 months of customer lifetime value vs hard-cancel. If Purovitalis is running monthly billing without a structured save-flow, the 20 to 30% first-month churn is recoverable revenue walking out the door.

Severity: Critical
03

Aura App + E-Comm Subscription Are Not Visibly Linked Into One Identity

A subscriber who takes a baseline Aura biomarker reading in month 1 and a follow-up in month 3 should be auto-served: a personalized progress email tied to which biomarkers moved, a recommended SKU expansion based on what didn't move, an at-risk save offer the moment biomarkers stagnate. From the outside, there is no visible mechanism wiring Aura's 50+ biomarker output into the subscription engine. This is the single largest strategic asset Purovitalis is sitting on and not yet weaponizing. Aura should be the retention machine, not just a marketing acquisition app.

Severity: High
04

Clinical Authority Signal Is Thin Relative to Premium Pricing

Purovitalis charges premium prices ($49.99 subscription on NAD+ Booster, $42.99 on Spermidine, $26.50 on Berberine). The price is justified by liposomal delivery, EU manufacturing, and third-party COAs. What is missing from the public surface is the named clinical authority: medical advisory board, named PhD researchers, peer-reviewed publication ties, named hospital or university collaborators. Koen Ridder's founder story (badminton athlete, personal health wake-up) is a strong personal narrative but does not by itself carry the clinical credibility a NAD+ stack needs against named-clinician competitors like Tru Niagen (ChromaDex), Timeline (Mitopure / Amazentis SA), or Renue By Science. Premium pricing without premium authority signal is structurally vulnerable as the category professionalizes.

Severity: High
05

No Visible Refill-Prediction or Auto-Replenishment Intelligence

A 30-capsule NMN bottle taken daily runs out on a known date. A smart subscription engine sends a refill reminder 5 days before run-out tied to actual consumption rate, not just the calendar. From the outside, the Purovitalis subscription appears to be calendar-billing only, no consumption-tied logic visible. The result: customers who skip a day occasionally either over-stock (then cancel because they have too much) or run out (then cancel because the brand "made me run out"). Either way, churn risk goes up. Consumption-tied subscription logic typically lifts retention 8 to 15 percentage points against straight calendar billing.

Severity: Medium-High
06

Medication-Interaction and Safety-Question Routing Is Manual

A meaningful percentage of supplement support contacts are safety questions: "I'm on metformin, can I take berberine," "I had thyroid surgery, is NMN okay," "I'm pregnant, what should I stop." These cannot be answered by a generic AI agent. Industry best practice: an AI triage layer routes safety questions to a trained human (or to a partner telehealth platform) while handling order-status and product-info questions autonomously. From the outside, there is no visible safety-question routing infrastructure. If a single safety-question response goes wrong, the regulatory and reputational cost is far larger than the labor savings of automation. This needs to be built right.

Severity: Medium-High
07

Review and UGC Generation Visible but Likely Under-Automated

Trustpilot at 4.6 / 5 with 412+ reviews is good, not category-leading (Tru Niagen sits at 10K+ reviews on Trustpilot, Timeline above 5K). The on-site testimonial reel is dense but uses repeated short blurbs ("More energy and strength at workouts" - Nadia), which signal manual aggregation, not a triggered automated post-purchase review request hitting at the 3-month consistency window (the moment customers feel the effect). An automated review-generation engine timed to the moment of perceived benefit typically 3 to 5x review velocity within 90 days.

Severity: Medium
08

Affiliate + Ambassador Programs Listed but Not Visibly Operationalized

Both an Affiliate Program and an Ambassador Program are linked from the explore menu. What is not visible from the outside: tiered affiliate commission structure, automated affiliate onboarding, ambassador content library, ambassador exclusive SKU drops, conversion tracking dashboards visible to affiliates. A longevity brand sitting on Koen Ridder's personal story plus the Aura biomarker app has unusually strong UGC potential (before-and-after biomarker screenshots are inherently shareable). The affiliate engine, properly operationalized, is the cheapest CAC channel a longevity brand can build.

Severity: Medium
09

Multi-Region Stack Fragmentation (EU + US + Mongolia + Distribution Partners)

Purovitalis operates an EU site, a US site (purovitalis.us), retail in Mongolia, and distribution partners in additional EU markets. From the outside, the EU site and US site appear to run on different e-commerce stacks. The Aura app likely speaks to one but not the other. A customer who buys NMN in the US and then re-engages via Aura should be one identity. A Mongolia retail customer who later subscribes online should be the same person. Multi-region brands consistently lose 5 to 12% of revenue to stack fragmentation, duplicate identities, inconsistent pricing, and customer-service routing failures across regions.

Severity: Medium

Revenue Opportunities

What Opens Up When the Gaps Close

Each opportunity below is the direct inverse of a bottleneck above. Close the gap, capture the margin.

01

AI Customer Service Layer (Chat + Email + Phone Triage)

Deploy an AI chat agent trained on the full Purovitalis product line, the FAQ surface, order-status integration, subscription management (pause, skip, swap SKU), and an explicit guardrail layer that routes any medication-interaction or pregnancy-related question to a trained human or partner telehealth platform. At industry-benchmark resolution (35 to 45% of contacts autonomous within 90 days), the labor savings alone justify the build. For a brand at Purovitalis scale, this is conservatively a 6-figure annual savings, before counting the conversion lift from sub-minute response time.

Impact: Very High
02

Structured Subscription Save-Flow Engine

Build a cascade: cancel intent triggers pause offer, then skip-a-month, then downsize SKU, then free Aura re-scan ("see what you've already moved"), then one-time discount, then clean cancel. Each step's conversion is tracked, optimized, and tied to the specific cancel reason captured. Industry benchmark: structured save-flows preserve up to 12 months of customer LTV per saved subscription. For a brand losing 20 to 30% of first-month subscribers, even a 30% save-flow conversion is a 7-figure annual revenue retention.

Impact: Very High
03

Aura-Tied Retention Intelligence Layer

Wire Aura biomarker output into the subscription engine. A subscriber who shows positive movement on cardiovascular biomarkers gets a celebration email and a Heart Health SKU expansion offer. A subscriber whose energy biomarker stagnates gets a "let's adjust your stack" offer for the NAD+ Booster. A subscriber who skips two consecutive scans gets a re-engagement flow. This is the single largest strategic moat Purovitalis can build, no other longevity DTC brand has wired a proprietary biomarker app into a subscription engine this tightly. The Aura app is wasted potential until it does this.

Impact: Very High
04

Clinical Authority Build-Out (Advisory Board + Named Researchers)

Recruit 2 to 4 named clinical advisors with PhD or MD credentials in metabolic, longevity, or nutritional biochemistry. Publish their bios. Tie specific products to named lab partners or named research. Build a "Science Council" or "Medical Review Board" page with photos and credentials. The brands above Purovitalis on the category retention chart (Tru Niagen, Timeline, NOVOS) all do this aggressively. Clinical authority is the lock that justifies premium pricing in the post-2026 longevity category.

Impact: High
05

Consumption-Tied Refill Prediction + Smart Reorder

Replace pure-calendar billing with consumption-tied logic. Customers can mark "I missed yesterday" in the app, the next refill auto-adjusts. Refill reminders fire 5 days before predicted run-out, not 30 days from last billing. Refill bundles are recommended ("you're due for NMN, want to add Resveratrol at 15% off?"). Conversion-tied refill engines typically lift retention 8 to 15 percentage points and grow average order value 12 to 25%.

Impact: High
06

Safety-Question Triage with Optional Telehealth Bolt-On

Build a guardrailed triage layer: AI handles "where is my order" and "what does spermidine do" without restriction. Anything that mentions medication, pregnancy, diagnosed condition, or specific health concern routes to a queued human reviewer or to a partner telehealth provider (Plush Care, Hims-style infrastructure). This is both a risk-reduction play (one wrong answer is regulatory exposure) and a revenue play (telehealth-attached supplement protocols command 2 to 3x retention).

Impact: Medium-High
07

Triggered Review-Generation Engine at 90-Day Mark

Automated review request fires at day 90 of consistent subscription, when the customer is most likely to have felt the effect (Purovitalis itself says "for best results, use for at least 3 months"). Request includes a one-click path to Trustpilot, Google, and a UGC video upload that can be auto-routed to the social team. This is the cheapest path to closing the Trustpilot-volume gap with Tru Niagen and Timeline.

Impact: Medium
08

Affiliate + Ambassador Operationalization

Build a real affiliate dashboard with tiered commissions, automated payouts, content library, conversion tracking, and an ambassador-exclusive SKU drop calendar. The Aura biomarker app gives affiliates and ambassadors uniquely shareable content (before-and-after biological-age screenshots). Properly operationalized, this is a paid-media CAC alternative at a fraction of the cost. Longevity DTC brands with strong affiliate engines are running 30 to 50% of total revenue through that channel.

Impact: Medium

What We'd Ask in the Diagnostic

These are the questions we can't answer from the outside. They determine the exact scope and priority of what gets deployed first.

Subscriber Base: How many active subscribers across EU + US? What is the active vs paused vs cancelled split? What's the average ACV per subscriber per year?

Churn Curves: What does month 1, month 3, month 6, and month 12 retention look like today by SKU? How does retention differ between the NMN-led cohorts and the NAD+ Booster-led cohorts?

Customer Service Volume: How many support contacts per week (email + chat + phone)? What's the rough category split: order status, product question, safety question, subscription action, complaint? Average response time today?

Save-Flow Today: When a customer hits cancel, what happens? Is there a pause or skip option offered? Is there cancel-reason capture? What's the current save rate?

Aura App Engagement: How many monthly active Aura users? What's the overlap between Aura users and active subscribers? Are Aura biomarker readings currently feeding any downstream marketing or retention logic?

Multi-Region Stack: Is purovitalis.com and purovitalis.us on the same backend or separate stacks? How does customer identity reconcile across regions? Who owns each region operationally?

Clinical Authority: Is there an existing advisory board, even informal? Named researchers Purovitalis has worked with? Lab partners willing to be publicly named? Path to building a Science Council in the next 6 months?

Decision Authority: Who internally owns the e-commerce stack? The Aura app roadmap? The retention strategy? What's been tried before on AI customer service or save-flow and why didn't it stick?
