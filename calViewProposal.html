03 — What's Costing You Right Now

The Paid Search Problems Solo Tinters Always Hit.

After auditing the OC tinting market and the way most solo operators run paid ads, here's what we consistently see leaking budget and slowing growth.

🔍

No Dedicated Google Ads Strategy

Most tinters either don't run Google Ads at all, or run them through an agency juggling Meta, TikTok, SEO, email and ten other platforms. Google Ads done part-time is Google Ads done badly. It's a specialist channel.

🌐

Homepage as Landing Page

Sending paid Google traffic to a generic Wix homepage is the #1 reason ad spend underperforms. The homepage answers "who is Calview Films?" — a paid landing page needs to answer "why book Calview Films right now?"

📊

No Conversion Tracking

If you can't tell which keyword turned into a $3,500 job, you're flying blind. Most tinting accounts have no proper conversion tracking — every campaign optimization is a guess instead of a decision.

🎯

Broad Match Keyword Bleed

Default Google Ads campaigns serve your ads for "window cleaning," "window replacement," "car window repair" — none of which you do. Without aggressive negative keyword management, 30–50% of your spend is on the wrong searches.

💰

Manual Bidding on Autopilot

Bids set once and forgotten. No bid adjustments by device, location, time of day, or audience. Commercial searches in Costa Mesa at 10am Tuesday should bid different than residential searches Sunday night. Most accounts treat them identical.

📝

Generic Ad Copy & Extensions

Ads that look like every other tinter's ads. No use of callouts, structured snippets, sitelinks, or call extensions. Quality Score suffers, CPC goes up, you pay more for less. Google rewards specificity — and almost nobody gives it specificity.

Ad Creation & Testing

Ongoing ad copy creation and testing — headlines, descriptions, callouts, structured snippets, sitelink extensions, and call extensions. Always at least two variants running per ad group to see what's actually winning.

Bidding & Budget Optimization

Bid strategies tuned based on actual conversion data — not Google's defaults. Bid adjustments by device, location, time of day, and audience. Budget reallocated monthly toward the campaigns and keywords producing real jobs.
