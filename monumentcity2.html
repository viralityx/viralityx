Section 01

What they actually do (in plain language)

Monument City Capital is two businesses under one brand, both run by Shaun Magner out of Glen Burnie, MD.

Business one (Borrow side): Hard money lender to real estate investors. Fix and flip loans, fix to rent, and 30-year DSCR rentals. They fund the down payment and rehab on investment properties for flippers and landlords across Maryland and beyond. Promise is 24-hour approval, 72-hour to close, no appraisal needed. Borrowers go through a third-party application portal (joinlendr.com). Typical loan size $150K to $500K. They make money on origination fees plus interest.

Business two (Invest side): They run an SEC Regulation D 506(c) Income Fund. Accredited investors put in $100K minimum, get monthly distributions targeting 10 to 11% annual return, 12-month commitment. The fund's capital is what gets deployed as the hard money loans. So the two sides feed each other.

About Shaun: Started in 2013 as an "accidental landlord," scaled into operations across acquisitions, redevelopment, property management, and now lending. He's been featured on multiple podcasts, was a keynote speaker at REI conferences, and spent years as a coach/speaker for one of the nation's top real estate investment education companies. That's the "coaching/consulting" piece CJ flagged. It's less a separate productized funnel right now, more authority infrastructure he's not fully leveraging.

Section 02

The strategic angle you lead with

This is a disciplined regional lender in a category that's structurally growing (hard money origination went from $50B in 2020 to $70-80B in 2026, on track for $100B). Macro is great. The problem is they're running both sides of their business (borrowers and accredited investors) through the same generic front door, with no visible automation for speed-to-quote, no repeat-borrower engine, and no broker portal. National platforms like Kiavi, RCN, and Stormfield are eating their potential market share by responding to inquiries faster and locking in brokers with better tools.

Frame it operator to operator: "You've got a real moat. Local Baltimore operations, your own redevelopment track record, founder credibility from years coaching. The nationals can't replicate that. What they CAN beat you on right now is speed. They get a quote out in 2 minutes, you respond in 4 hours, you lose the deal. That's the gap I want to walk you through."

Section 07

Objection prep (rehearse these once)

"We already have Lendr handling our applications."

You: Lendr is a great loan management tool, that's not what we replace. Lendr handles the application form and the loan file. We sit in front of Lendr and behind it. In front: we make sure the borrower who fills out that Lendr form first got a soft pre-qual from you in under 2 minutes so they commit to you instead of shopping. Behind: we make sure every closed loan triggers a 12 to 18 month nurture sequence that gets you the next loan from that same borrower. Lendr doesn't do either of those. Different layer entirely.

"My borrower flow is mostly word-of-mouth and brokers, I don't need more leads."

You: Even better. We're not a lead-gen agency, we don't push more leads at you. What we do is convert MORE of the leads you already have. If you're already getting good word-of-mouth and broker flow but you're losing 30% of those to Kiavi or RCN because they get back faster with a number, that's the gap we fix. Same flow, higher conversion, more revenue per inquiry. Plus we build a broker portal so the brokers you DO have stay loyal instead of getting recruited away.

"I'm not sure AI is a fit for a lending business, this is a relationship game."

You: Completely agree it's a relationship game. That's exactly why AI fits. AI doesn't replace the relationship, it makes sure the borrower or the investor never falls through the cracks BEFORE you get a chance to build the relationship. Right now if Shaun is on a call with one borrower at 11am and another borrower calls about a deal that closes Friday, that second borrower hits voicemail and goes to a competitor. The AI catches that call, gets the deal specs, and tees Shaun up to call back warm at 2pm. Relationship intact, deal saved.

"How much does this cost?"

You: Depends on what your operation actually needs, and the borrower side versus investor side are very different builds. That's why the Blueprint Review with Daniel is the right next step. He'll walk you through what we'd build, in what order, and you'll get a real number against your real situation. Pricing without context just sounds like a number you'd argue with.

"Just send me the audit and I'll take a look."

You: Happy to. I'll send it right now. The audit is sharp on its own but the real value is the 20 minutes where Daniel walks through your specific numbers live, especially the speed-to-quote piece and the Income Fund nurture, those are nuanced. After you've read it, want me to lock you in for one of his slots this week? Even a tentative hold. If it lands flat after you read, easy to cancel.
